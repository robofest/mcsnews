Spring Summer 2008

The 16th IGVC (Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition) was held on May 30-June 2 at Oakland University. LTU MCS Viper team won 2nd place award in autonomous challenge that is the main competition of IGVC. Cash prize for the 2nd place is $2,500. 47 teams from 39 Universities participated in this year’s competition. LTU Viper also won the JAUS (Joint Architecture for Unmanned Systems) level 3 award. Only two teams got the award of which cash prize is $500. The other University is Hosei University that is one of the top 4 Universities in Japan. 39th Annual LTU High School Mathematics Competition Winners

 

The Mathematics and Computer Science Department at Lawrence Technological University is pleased to announce the results of the 39th Annual LTU High School Mathematics Competition, held on April 27, 2008. The competition is one of America's longest running high school mathematics competitions. This year, over fifty of southeastern Michigan's brightest mathematics students competed for prizes of US savings bonds. The results:

 

First Place:        Matthew Vengalil          10th Grade        Grosse Pointe North

 

Second Place:    Ram Bhaskar                10th Grade        International Academy

 

Third Place:       Robin He                      10th Grade        Ann Arbor Huron

 

Honorable Mention (listed alphabetically):

                       

    Randy Jia                       9th Grade        Detroit Country Day School

 

    Mason Liang                  9th Grade        Troy High School

 

    Sudharshan Mohanram 11th Grade        Novi High School

 

The competition was authored by Professor Michael Merscher of LTU, and covers high school mathematics up to but not including calculus. No calculators are allowed, so mathematical competence and creativity are required to answer the challenging questions. The competition is held each year in late April on the Lawrence Tech campus.

Master of Science in Computer Science - Program Description

The Master of Science in Computer Science differs from traditional master's degree programs in that the emphasis is be on applied concepts and on reinforcing these applications in laboratories. Examples are be drawn from business and industry, finance, and scientific research. The program has been structured so that students will be able to select structured sequences of options that will give them advanced training in certain limited fields of computer science. A choice of four specific areas of concentration will be available. The student is required to complete two of them:
 

  • Software Engineering--This option is designed to allow students to concentrate on the development methodology of programming and testing in the large. It emphasizes understanding of user requirements and the implementation of software to meet user needs.
  • Knowledge-Based Systems--This option allows students to practice and explore specific applications of Artificial Intelligence methodologies (expert systems, inference, representation of knowledge) to problems in manufacturing and finance.
  • Data Base Systems--This option gives students advanced training and experience in working with distributed data base systems and client/server models.
  • Distributed Systems--This option introduces students to the theoretical underpinnings of distributed computing and practical implementations of LANS, WANS, Internets and Intranets.

ADMISSION

Admission to the M.S.C.S. requires:
1. A baccalaureate degree that includes one year of mathematics and one year of science;
2. An official transcript of all completed college work;
3. An overall undergraduate GPA of at least 3.00 on a 4.00 scale;
4. Undergraduate coursework or professional experience equivalent to the following pre-core sequence of             undergraduate courses at LTU.
    MCS1514 Computer Science 1
    MCS2514 Computer Science 2
    MCS2524 Discrete Math
    MCS2534 Data Structures
    MCS3513 Assembler
    MCS3653 Computer Architecture
    MCS4653 Automata Theory
    MCS4663 Operating Systems

Students not fulfilling the requirements  may be provisionally admitted to the program.

DEGREE REQUIREMENTS

The 30-credit-hour program contains:

A) Core Courses
Students must complete sequences in two areas of concentration for a total of 18 credits. Each sequence is comprised of two courses and one seminar.

    1. Software Engineering (9 credits total)
        MCS5103 Introduction to Software Engineering
        MCS6123 Advanced Software Engineering Techniques
        MCS6143 Current Developments in Software Engineering
    2. Database Systems (9 credits total)
        MCS5303 Introduction to Database Systems
        MCS6323 Distributive Database Systems
        MCS6343 Current Developments in Database Systems
    3. Knowledge-Based Systems (9 credits total)
        MCS5503 Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
        MCS6523 Expert Systems
        MCS6543 Current Developments in Artificial Intelligence
    4. Distributed Computing (9 credits total)
        MCS5703 Introduction to Distributed Computing
        MCS6723 Advanced Topics in Distributed Computing
        MCS6743 Current Developments in Distributed Computing

B) Elective Courses
Two elective courses for a total of 6 credits.

        MCS5203 Computer Graphics 
        Intranet and Internet Technologies
        MCS5023 Java Programming 
        Selected Electrical Engineering Electives
        Selected Management Electives
        Software Project Management
        Visual Programming

C) Capstone Project
Capstone project for a total of 6 credits.

        MCS7013 Collaborative Research Project 1
        MCS7033 Collaborative Research Project 2

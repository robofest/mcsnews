Department of Computer Science

Intelligent Systems and Autonomous Robotics Lab

Lab Objectives

·        Provide hands-on, even “bodies-on” space for MCS undergraduate and graduate courses

·        Provide state-of-the-arts facility for Intelligent Systems and Autonomous Robotics research focusing on Artificial Intelligence, Fuzzy Logic Control, Evolutionary Computing, Artificial Neural-nets, and Soft-computing

·        Promote robotics related events such as RoboFest, International RoboFest, Robot Summer School, and Summer Oddyssey

·        Provide learning environment for robotics classes for Schoolteachers

·        Build, test, collect, and display Autonomous Robots for a possible Robotics Museum

·        Improve visibility of LTU as a Technology Leader

Lab Equipments

  • 3 LTU laptops

  • 3 Khepera robots

  • 15 Lego Mindstorms

  • 1 TINI board and socket

  • Four MIT Handy Board Robot Kits
